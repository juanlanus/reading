The average of all averages is 3.52, somehow positive as it is over the theoretical average of 2.52.

The performance of the test was affected by some issues of the testing instructions and defects of the UI proper.

The plan for the future is to consider this test series a pilot, fix the issues, and roll out a new series of many tests with varies subjects (as this UI is intended to be used by anybody).

findings

Most of the findings came from the observation of the subjects.

  • [1] The meaning of the icons is not as obvious for non-geeks.
  • [2] In particular, the less obvious item with the icons was that they have to be clicked once again in order to revert their effect.
  • [3] One issue with the test design is that the subjects need to read too much and remember a number of steps. The instructions are lengthy.
  • [4] The "control panel" (the vertical bar at the left of the window) is intrusive.
  • [5] It was not clear that the hihglighting works by selecting the text first and then clicking the button. A user was used to do it the other way: selecting a highlight tool and then applying it to the text.
  • [6] The transparency of the sliding panels make them difficult to read, the users read slowly and have issues to scan for a word like "Microsoft"

changes

  • [1]: Make new icons, crafted by a read graphics designer.
  • [2]: At the first click of a button that slides a panel, change the state of the UI in order for the buttons to show that clicking again will "un-slide" that panel. Maybe with an arrow (a triangle as in the slide show controls) to indicate forward or back movement of the panel.
  • [3]: Rewrite the consent form and the instructions to make them brief, about 50% of their current size.
  • [3]: Make clear that the instructions are available all along the test by clicking the menu button (that according to [1] is not easily identifiable) possibly by showing a callout when the test page is opened.
  • [3]: Make a screencast showing the basic operations, to save the subject from reading an abstract explanation.
  • [4]: Make the control panel slowly faint until it's almost invisible, all but the progress indicator. Reshow it when used, or on hover for desktops.
  • [4]: Make the control panel narrower.
  • [4]: Make the control panel (almost) the same color as the text background.
  • [4]: For mobile devices set the control panel as a horizontal bar (instead of vertical) at the bottom of the screen when in portrait mode.
  • [5]: Make the highlighting work the other way too: clicking first in the highlight button turns it into a highlight tool that highlights the text as it is selected. Need to decide how unload the cursor.
  • [6]: Make the sliding panels 100% opaque.
