Usability Test Instructions

The Books Smart Reader

Please perform the following steps:

  • If you are using Internet Explorer then don't waste your time: this won't work, it will fail miserabily (as of IE8). Thanks anyway.
  • Take note that the test is not on you, but on the system. If you experience problems it's not your fault, it's because we aren't done honing the system's UI. Hence this test. Your critic feedback is very valuable for us, much more than a patronizong "very nice".
  • We are thankful with those who help us taking this test. If you left your email, and this project takes off, we'll let you know.

  • Using the Chrome browser, open the following page: http://ec2-54-227-184-202.compute-1.amazonaws.com:8080/tlotfc/tlotfc2.html

  • Wait for a while, until the page fully loads. It is hosted in a free facility, which is not the fastest, kindly provided by Amazon Web Services.
  • Display the help text by clicking the question mark (?) icon and read the Operation of the UI section (where "UI" stands for "User Interface").
  • Optionally, if you want increased insight, read the other part of the help text, intitled "Purpose and description of the application". You are welcome.
  • When you are done dimiss the help panel by clicking the help button again.

  • You don't need to remember the instructions in full, during the test they will be available by clicling the menu icon. Click once to show the instructions, click again to hide them.

  • Smart scroll test:

    After having clicked the Continue button, read all or part of the article that shows for about 10 minutes, more if you want. It's intitled "The Loneliness Of The Female Coder" and is about sexism in software development workspaces.

    To progress in the reading use the smart scroll feature, that is, advance the text ("scroll") by pressing the space bar and use shift + space bar to back up.

    The idea is to use the smart scroll feature enough time as to get used to it.

  • Progress indicator

    Notice the progress indicator in the top left corner of the screen and check how it changes as you read.

  • TOC

    Using the TOC (table of content) locate a title that mentions "Microsoft" and select it.

  • Highlighting

    Select a piece of text (by dragging the mouse over them) and highlight then with the yellow highlight button.

    Select several pieces of text (holding down the "Ctrl" key) and highlight them with a single click of the highlight button.

  • Finish

    Once you are done, or if you don't want to continue, click the "End" button to proceed to the survey about the test results.
